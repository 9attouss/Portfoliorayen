I'm Mohamed Rayen Akrout, a self-taught video editor. I didn't learn this craft in a classroom — I learned it by opening the software, making mistakes, and figuring out why a cut works before I could name the technique.

That path keeps me curious. I move across short-form, narrative, branded, and music video work because I like learning the rhythm each format demands, rather than settling into one lane too early.

I'm early in my career, but every project is treated as the next rung — a chance to tighten pacing, sharpen sound design, and get closer to cuts that disappear into the story instead of calling attention to themselves.
